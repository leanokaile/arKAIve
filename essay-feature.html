Cracks on the UniTeam's Sandglass: Why Now?.

Essay • 2026 • arKAIve Collection

The Country's political landscape has been shaken by the recent breakup of the UniTeam electoral alliance, a coalition that once seemed indestructible. The alliance, which brought together President Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. and Vice President Sara Duterte, was a powerhouse in the 2022 elections. According to Senator Imee Marcos, she personally convinced VP Duterte to be her brother's running mate, which is why she swears to do anything to salvage the said alliance alongside other supporters of the UniTeam. But now, the cracks have become too deep to ignore, and the alliance has officially disbanded. Others doubt if there was ever a unity at the very beginning. The question on everyone's mind is: why now?

The UniTeam alliance was formed out of strategic necessity, bringing together two influential political families to secure a decisive victory in the 2022 elections. Their campaign was built on a platform of unity, promising to bridge political divides and bring stability to the nation. However, the foundation of this alliance had always been wobbly, with political analysts predicting significant challenges due to the differing political camps of Marcos and Duterte.

The first signs of trouble emerged when Vice President Duterte resigned from her Cabinet posts in the Marcos administration. This move, which came just two years after their successful campaign, was a clear indication that all was not well within the UniTeam. Duterte's resignation from her roles as the Secretary of Education and the vice chairperson of the National Task Force to End Local Communist Armed Conflict (NTF-ELCAC) sent shockwaves through the political landscape. She adds further that "We are not candidates anymore," suggesting an input that the alliance was only ever for the elections.

The tension between the two leaders became even more apparent when Duterte refused to mention President Marcos' middle name, Romualdez, in a public speech. This seemingly small act was a symbolic gesture that highlighted the growing rift between the two leaders.

The final blow came when Duterte publicly criticized the House of Representatives and Speaker Martin Romualdez, a close ally of President Marcos. Duterte accused Romualdez of politicking and positioning himself for a presidential run in 2028, a claim that Romualdez denied. This public spat further exposed the deep divisions within the said alliance.

So, why is this breakup trending now? The timing of these events is crucial. All political affairs is gearing up for the 2025 midterm elections, and the breakup of the UniTeam alliance has created a power vacuum that could reshape the political landscape. Political observers and fresh minds from students among different universities are now watching closely to see how this breakup will affect the upcoming elections and whether new alliances will emerge to fill the void.

For now, the public nature of the breakup, with high-profile resignations and public criticisms, has captured the attention of the media and the public. The drama and intrigue surrounding the breakup have made it a trending topic, with people eager to see how the story unfolds.
