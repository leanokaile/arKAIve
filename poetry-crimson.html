Crimson

Poetry • arKAIve Collection
"I miss you" but of course, I'm not gonna say that. instead, I'll itch my fingers like a busybody, pretending I'm too busy to even notice your shadow. My skin would begin to peel with every scratch, and I wouldn't be able to feel it until the first drop of water lands. But no matter the burn, it does not—in any way—account for the tension beneath my ribcage: a sudden ache my body remembers, the way my mind insists on forgetting. It was never about me demanding the love I couldn't have; it was the love I had that overflowed, even without anyone preparing a bucket to catch it. So if I scratch myself and bleed, I'll swoon at the sight of crimson and thank God I get to bleed.
