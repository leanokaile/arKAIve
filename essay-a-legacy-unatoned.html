A Legacy Unatoned

In the spirit of all courts in any judicial system, we see Lady Justice in a long robe and a blindfold, carrying her scale and sword, sparing no impurities to break through her grasp. The things she carried weren't for the props or any special effects. It was to show that through her blindfold, justice is served impartially and unbiased; free from any external factors. With her scale, she equally gives both sides a consideration to be weighed to see which is more compelling depending on the evidence presented. With her sword, she commands authority, imposing that there is a penalty to be received by those who dares to violate the law. She wore the robe, often in white and draping around her feet, to show that she is free from any corruption and impurities as justice is far reaching. This is to say that whatever I deem as "just" does not truly suffice as to whether it is "truly just". With regards to Enrile's case, I would like to offer a question on the table: On which court will he be truly judged? The heavens or on Earth?

Juan Ponce Enrile has left lasting marks on the Philippines as his legacy — one that is unpleasant and ever haunting even as we speak. He was one of the architects of Martial Law, admitting that the ambush made on his way home that one unfortunate September in 1972, was all but a true ambush. It was staged just as everything was scripted to become a national performance in order to garner wealth and control from the people. He had been involved in numerous accounts of corruption and illegal businesses such as the Timber License Agreement, where he was able to fully regenerate his wealth at the cost of the country's own Flora. Not to mention his involvement in the Coco Levy scam with Danding Cojuangco as well as the Pork Barrel Scam. After all that, he leaves the Earth without true accountability, even lasted in the judicial system only to end up as one of the current President's legal advisers. He lived long enough to be able to atone for his legal wrongdoings, but still failed to do so.

If I were to be asked where the justice lies in this situation, I would say that it never reached Enrile; however, it will be on his deathbed as no one will ever mourn the wicked. He may have left devastating effects on the country, shortening the lives of thousands of people and cutting the opportunities for economic development, but he will never have the one most priceless thing to ever occur in the universe: eternal peace. His soul shall be damned regardless whether heaven or hell truly exists. Even in death, he will be known as a very tainted persona in Philippine History. As Lady Justice stands still on her court, the scale will be heavier on the side of the people who mourn for the lost thousands. Enrile shall be struck by her sword, on repeat, as an everlasting punishment. If the court was unable to rule on the people’s behalf when his vulnerable body is still intact, then he shall perish on the other side, assuming it is real. Even if it isn’t, I'll be one of those who will make sure that his foul legacy will live on and shall not go forgotten. I will make sure that anyone who's directly involved with him will have their names drenched in shame and disgust. Once I rise in society and fully solidify my role as a citizen who can make tangible changes, I shall be his reckoning, and so will be my current generation. And this reckoning is only the beginning.
