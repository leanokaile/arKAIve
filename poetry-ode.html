An Ode to the Cycle of Learning

Poetry • arKAIve Collection
I come to you not as a woman, but as an entity wrapped in white sheets clean as a blank canvas, draped in sterile cloth and etched in the book of atlas. Like an entity claiming its first breath on earth, your rays would be the first sky I'd ever seen. I lie beneath the moist ground—barely awake, and my only hope in life had put my mother to her grave. So subtly did the life and nature came to fuse, your rays would be the first I considered my muse. The canvas was with color in the book i often refuse, which soon i learned that it was of big use. As if on cue, the rays were no longer blinding. The pages were sweet as a cane, colorful and charming; a cycle that seems to be neverending. It was when I came to realize that I consume the rays like oxygen. A moment elapsed, and then I am a woman bringing life again. The neverending loop delivered my entity, surprising that it did not cause the end of me. The natural laws of life had always been gravity's greatest envy. Truth came in rays passed onto me similarly To how my mother presented her legacy. And it was the light of truth in knowledge that led me to unlock the world's endless possibilities. Finally, my time as an entity came to cease. The rays shall work against my body with ease. I nod at my ending, for the truth was a beauty only I may now seize.
