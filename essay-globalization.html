Globalization: A National Breakthrough

Essay • 2026 • arKAIve Collection

The borderlines in communication may come in a variety of forms, either tangible or intangible. As the world further develops within the advancements in technology, globalization comes out as a door, which opens opportunities for many citizens; a way to break through the borderlines. Countries have never been more connected to each other just as communication travels nearly as fast as light. Globalization, in fact, cannot be easily defined as its functions will be limited. Different perspectives on this concept have also been included, adding up to the reasons why globalization is yet to be defined with finality. But in a more general term, globalization does not only tap into the use of language and sharing of social ways on how to communicate. Its purpose covers a wide field that tends to converge with a diverse community; therefore, opening new perspectives. Communication, as discussed in the previous lectures, is the transmission of messages in a medium, to be delivered to a receiver, which may also come in different processes. However, it must be taken into account that both parties have to be open for proper communication to happen. Just like a bottle, transferring its contents to another container. This correlates to globalization in a way that communication serves as the starting point of the former. Without this, globalization would have ceased before it even started.

For all things to happen, particularly Globalization, three known drivers were described, namely: colonization, diaspora, and technologies being the most prevalent in the modern times. Starting with colonization, Philippine history is filled with forceful influences mainly from the Spaniards, the Japanese, and the Westerners. All of which has influences that are still existing up to this day. Diaspora, on the other hand, leans toward the idea of migration as Filipinos would move to another country to either work or settle into a new citizenship. I'd be lying if I said I don't wish to travel and move my family to a more prosperous country. What's more prevalent today, as mentioned above, is technology. The accessibility of this development is outstanding that almost every citizen in the country is a registered owner of a gadget, may it be a mobile phone, laptop, tablet, television, or any household appliances that can break the invisible wall between boundaries or other countries. This is a must-have these days due to the consistent upgrading standards in general. Upon the breakthrough, comes a set of pros and cons that affects how people within a certain area communicate with each other. As globalization presents new opportunities, it also elevates people's social and financial standing by creating connections on either a smaller or a larger scale. Its downside is that the shared information or thought can be easily mimicked for one party's advantage, leaving the other party unattended. This creates a significant imbalance in the overall development of the country, hence, the divide between the rich and the poor. Another good influence of globalization in communication is the ongoing cooperation among nations. Communication, itself, is powerful enough to catalyze a peace and a war between nations. With globalization, holding proper communication, countries involved in a negotiation can both benefit in an agreement, without exploiting each other's strengths. But with every advancements made in technology, it possesses a potential danger to its own citizens, not to mention all of the affected users in a closer proximity. Additionally, global wealth soared beyond bars, leaving a mass production of carbon footprint that fuels global warming. The goods in the effects of globalization have been unconsciously taken for granted, degrading the sole purpose of good communication, which is to connect and settle into a mutual understanding.

Without a doubt, the global society is in need of recalibration to reach a mutual understanding for effective communication. In order to do so, those that are involved must know the following: keep it simple, eliminate technical jargons, be concise but not too concise, avoid abbreviations and acronyms, reduce the use of canned phrases, be concrete, and be conversational. The four are distinct but have a common ground. When we communicate thoughts and messages, we must also consider the situation of the receiver, which relates to keeping it simple and straightforward (concrete), eliminating jargons, so that the receiver can fully grasp the sender's message--but not being too simple that the whole context cannot be understood without further explanations. This beats the thought of being "concise". Abbreviations should also be avoided unless it is a common term used in a general conversation. Reducing the use of canned phrases is also a must, given that the responses should be cohesive to avoid misunderstandings. Finally, being conversational is a means to engaging in a good conversation. Most people forget that the subject at hand is supposed to be directed towards a specific person, not only to themselves.

Quite frankly, the more I know about communication, the more I realize that I still have so much to learn. So, it's either I entertain the idea and be open, or I stick to my pride and set my own boundaries. My objective in life is simple: to be extremely beautiful and extremely educated and thoughtful. One way to do that is to become a good and responsible communicator.
