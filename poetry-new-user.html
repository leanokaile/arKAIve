The woman looking back at the camera looked like me, but I refuse to believe it was. --- My eyes open every time it strikes 3:59 a.m. on my wall clock. Like clockwork, I get up with the weight of the world dragging me down. This happens every day, even without an alarm going off—as if my mind is wired to reboot at that hour. My new laptop has been sitting adjacent to my bed, providing a clear view of myself. Perfect furniture blocking for when I choose to take a nap after locking in to a study session. And just like clockwork, its battery is completely drained every morning. Last week, I decided to plug it in overnight so I could use it the next day, but it didn't help. I was convinced my new device was just a piece of junk. I had it fixed immediately, but the shop owner didn't find anything wrong. It was in perfect condition, they said. My laptop is now on its third week of the same routine: dead battery at dawn, but perfectly functional after being recharged. So I did some thorough exploring on it. All was well until I saw the recent timestamps in my computer's gallery—pictures taken at exactly 1:14 a.m., 2:13 a.m., and 3:03 a.m. The woman looking back at the camera looked like me, but I refuse to believe it was. She's peeking out from the other side of the bed. Her eyes and mouth are dark and hollow. The corners of her lips strain desperately toward her ears. The next photo showed my back facing the camera, just sitting on my bed, doing nothing. The third one almost made my soul jump out of my body. It was a photo of me stitching clean, linear wounds on my back, all while looking directly at the camera—as if she were using it as a mirror. I had thought the stings I felt were mere bug bites. I scratched at them multiple times last week, slowly peeling my skin with my fingernails. My hand instinctively reaches to my back, only to find my skin smooth and free from any damage. Was it really me in the photos? My head throbs as I continue to scan the rest of them on my screen—all with the same timestamps, going back about three weeks. A variety of things I don't usually do keep showing up. Every morning, I wake up more tired than before. It's like something else is using me while I sleep. And tonight, for the first time, I'm afraid to fall asleep. Because whatever she's doing while I'm gone, she's getting better at pretending to be me.
